Some thoughts & questions, in semi-coherent order

November 9, 2020

Thoughts & questions I had before I started Codecademy:

  • Is it too late to change my project?
  • What’s that? It’s time to work on my project, you say? Nope, time to clean/eat a snack/do other work/literally do anything else.
  • I will do ANYTHING to not start this right now.
  • I’ll start next week!

Thoughts & questions I have as I convince myself to FINALLY get started:

  • I’m tired of talking about how much I don’t want to start. LET ME JUST GET STARTED.
  • Wow, the sign-up process was really easy.
  • I just gave my credit card information to Codeacdemy. They’re about to charge me $40 in SEVEN DAYS – so I better test them out. I better find out if they’re the learning platform that I want to go with.

Thoughts & questions I have as I go through my first lesson:

  • They make it really easy to start. There’s no inner emotional Shakespearean monologue like the one I cycle through in my head every time I even think about starting this project.
  • OMG THERE’S CODE ON THE FIRST PAGE. What’s that all about. What do I do.
    • But also: I need to seriously chill myself out. Let me give this platform a shot, and if it’s not working for me, let me move onto something else. You’ve spent weeks psyching yourself out about even starting your project – just take the first step, see how that feels, and then move onto the next step.
  • I STARTED! I did the first thing. I have no idea how many more lessons I have left to go, but it feels like it’ll pale in comparison to how long it took me to get started.
  • What’s the difference between HTML and CSS?
    • After Googling the difference: HOLD UP: do I have to learn CSS too?!
  • Thanks to HTML5 UP for the design template, but the coding was ALL ME, baby
